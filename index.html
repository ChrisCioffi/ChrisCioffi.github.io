Welcome

My name is Chris Cioffi. I am a freelance journalist and master's student at the University of Maryland in College Park.

I have worked for newspapers in Billings, Montana, Lynchburg, Virginia and Raleigh, North Carolina. I served as an Americorps volunteer in Billings and Fairbanks, Alaska before beginning my journalism career.

I am most interested in development topics where policy and people intersect, and hope to pursue a journalism career in Washington D.C. after graduation. If you like what you read, please send an email or message me on Twitter.

My Work
Their water is contaminated, and Wake residents want help
Janice Whitley’s husband, Bennie, loved to brag about the quality of the well water at their home just outside the city limits of east Raleigh. He probably would have been surprised to know the water wasn’t as pure as he thought.
Raleigh schools see increase in test scores, graduation rates
A data analysis of the Wake County School System state exam scores during the 2015-16 school year. The numbers showed growth in pass rates across the entire school system.
This building was bought by a mysterious investor. But nobody moved in
The James F. Battin Federal Building was sold by the GSA to Washington-based Colorado Tire Corp. Years later, it's still empty and people want answers.
2 years and counting: Crane marks spot of project in limbo
In June 2015, a crane went up along Hillsborough Street across from N.C. State University to help builders erect a 7-story apartment building. Nearly two years later, the crane is still there, but the building isn’t.
Teen who drowned was creative and adventurous
Rachel Rosoff was a quirky, curious teenager who acted in an improv troupe, wrote cheerful songs and dreamed of writing for “Saturday Night Live.” But the 17-year-old's life was cut short when she was shocked and drowned in a pool accident.
Local Farmers help meet Chesapeake Bay runoff regulations
A look at how farmers are adapting to Environmental Protection Agency regulations restricting runoff from wastewater treatment and farm and construction runoff to the Chesapeake Bay.
Can 2 Fried Chicken Festivals coexist in North Carolina?
Legislation to recognize a Fayetteville celebration as the official NC fried chicken festival caused an uproar in Rose Hill, which has the longstanding Poultry Jubilee. Both towns have deep ties to the large state poultry industry.
Senior Citizens in the oil patch can't affort rent
At the height of the oil boom, senior citizens living in the oil-boom town of Sidney were paying more in rent than people in New York City. They have been adapting by moving into subsidized housing.
