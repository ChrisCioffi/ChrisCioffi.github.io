A Visual Look at Our Favorite Movies
BY Chris Cioffi
There are movies we think are good and movies we all pay to go see. These movies are not always the same. This is a visual look at our favorite movies, using IMDB ratings and data of box office ticket sales (adjusted for inflation) from Box Office Mojo.
Each decade, one film stood out
Each decade between the 1920s and today, a movie commanded a big lead when it came to gross ticket sales.
Films in the 1990s and 2000s ruled
There were some great movies released in the 1990s and early 2000s. According to the IMDB database, nearly 40 percent of all the movies in the IMDB top 250 were released in those two decades.
Audiences love movies across the globe
All over the world, audiences head to movie theaters to take in a good flick. Below is a map of spending on films per 1,000 people. Per-capita, Australia spends the most.

Directors have a knack for blockbusters
Several directors knew the right formula to make a box office success as well as a critical darling. Seven directors were at the helm of films that not only made the IMDB Top 250 movies list, but also were on the list of top-grossing films.
The force is with Star Wars Films
Some Star Wars films, especially the prequels were were rated poorly on IMDB. But, even with bad reviews, people went to see them. All Star Wars films decidedly made fistfuls of cash.

Audiences vote for Mickey Mouse
Disney, and Buena Vista one of its brand names, have raked in nearly 20 percent of all the revenue for films in the Box Office Mojo top 200. The gross revenue was ranked by inflation and adjusted domestic gross ticket sales.
